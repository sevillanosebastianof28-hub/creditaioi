# Dispute Letter Generation Rules

**Document Priority: HIGH**
**Last Updated: 2025-01-29**
**Type: Document Standards**

---

## 1. APPROVED PHRASING

### 1.1 Opening Statements
**Approved:**
- "I am writing to dispute the following information in my credit report."
- "Pursuant to the Fair Credit Reporting Act, I am requesting an investigation of the following item(s)."
- "I am exercising my rights under 15 U.S.C. § 1681i to dispute inaccurate information."
- "Please investigate the following account, which I believe contains inaccurate information."

### 1.2 Dispute Reason Statements
**Approved:**
- "This account contains inaccurate information, specifically: [detail]"
- "The date of first delinquency reported does not match my records."
- "I do not recognize this account and believe it may have been opened fraudulently."
- "The balance reported is incorrect. My records show: [amount]"
- "This account has been reported beyond the 7-year reporting period."

### 1.3 Evidence References
**Approved:**
- "Enclosed please find documentation supporting my dispute."
- "I have attached copies of [documents] for your review."
- "The enclosed evidence demonstrates the inaccuracy of the reported information."
- "Supporting documentation is provided herewith."

### 1.4 Request Statements
**Approved:**
- "Please investigate this matter and correct or delete the inaccurate information."
- "I request that you conduct a reasonable investigation as required by law."
- "Please provide me with the results of your investigation in writing."
- "If you cannot verify this information, please delete it from my credit file."

### 1.5 Closing Statements
**Approved:**
- "Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter."
- "I look forward to receiving the results of your investigation within the statutory timeframe."
- "Please send written confirmation of your findings."
- "I appreciate your compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act."

## 2. FORBIDDEN WORDING

### 2.1 Threatening Language
**Never Include:**
- "I will sue you if you don't..."
- "My attorney will be contacting you..."
- "You will face legal consequences..."
- "I demand immediate action or else..."
- "You are breaking the law and will pay..."

### 2.2 Accusatory Language
**Never Include:**
- "You are lying about..."
- "This is fraudulent reporting..."
- "You are deliberately harming..."
- "Your company is corrupt..."
- "This is criminal behavior..."

### 2.3 Guarantee Claims
**Never Include:**
- "This must be removed because..."
- "You have no choice but to delete..."
- "The law requires you to remove..."
- "I know this will be deleted..."

### 2.4 Emotional Language
**Never Include:**
- "I am devastated by..."
- "This has ruined my life..."
- "How dare you..."
- "I can't believe you would..."
- "This is outrageous..."

## 3. STRUCTURE REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Required Elements
Every dispute letter must include:
1. **Consumer Information**
   - Full legal name
   - Current address
   - Social Security Number (last 4 digits acceptable)
   - Date of birth

2. **Account Identification**
   - Creditor/company name
   - Account number (full or partial)
   - Type of account
   - Reported balance

3. **Specific Dispute**
   - Clear statement of what is being disputed
   - Specific inaccuracy identified
   - Supporting reason for dispute

4. **Request for Action**
   - Clear request for investigation
   - Request for written results
   - Timeframe acknowledgment (optional)

5. **Signature Block**
   - Consumer signature
   - Date
   - Printed name

### 3.2 Document Format
- Professional business letter format
- Single-spaced with double-space between paragraphs
- 1-inch margins
- Professional font (Times New Roman, Arial)
- 11-12 point font size
- Include date prominently
- Use certified mail reference number if applicable

## 4. NEUTRAL TONE ENFORCEMENT

### 4.1 Tone Requirements
All letters must be:
- Factual and objective
- Professional and business-like
- Clear and concise
- Respectful while assertive
- Free of emotional language
- Based on verifiable claims

### 4.2 Tone Examples
**Wrong:** "I am outraged that you continue to report this false information that is destroying my credit!"

**Right:** "I am disputing the following information, which I believe to be inaccurate. Please investigate and correct this matter."

**Wrong:** "You people clearly don't care about consumers and just want to ruin lives."

**Right:** "I am exercising my rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act to request an investigation of this item."

## 5. NO-THREAT LANGUAGE STANDARDS

### 5.1 Assertion Without Threat
**Acceptable assertion:**
- "Please conduct a reasonable investigation as required by FCRA Section 611."
- "I expect this matter to be addressed within the statutory timeframe."
- "If this information cannot be verified, it should be deleted per FCRA requirements."

**Unacceptable threat:**
- "Investigate this or face legal action."
- "I will report you to every agency possible."
- "My lawyer is ready to file suit."

### 5.2 Consequence Language
When mentioning consumer rights:
- Focus on what the law provides
- Avoid personal threat framing
- State rights factually

**Example:**
"I understand that under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, if disputed information cannot be verified, it must be deleted from my credit file."

## 6. LEGAL CITATION GUIDELINES

### 6.1 Approved Citations
- FCRA 15 U.S.C. § 1681 (general)
- FCRA § 1681i (dispute investigation)
- FCRA § 1681e(b) (accuracy requirements)
- FCRA § 1681s-2 (furnisher duties)
- FCRA § 605 (information exclusions)
- FDCPA 15 U.S.C. § 1692g (debt validation)

### 6.2 Citation Usage
- Reference relevant sections
- Do not over-cite
- Do not misrepresent what law requires
- Use citations to support factual claims

**Good use:**
"Under FCRA Section 605(a), negative information generally may not be reported after seven years from the date of first delinquency."

**Poor use:**
"Section 611 REQUIRES you to DELETE this item!!"

## 7. TEMPLATE CATEGORIES

### 7.1 Required Template Types
- Basic dispute letter (general inaccuracy)
- DOFD dispute letter (date issues)
- Balance dispute letter
- "Not my account" dispute letter
- Collection validation request
- Post-7-year deletion request
- Inquiry removal request
- Duplicate account dispute
- Goodwill adjustment request (separate category)

### 7.2 Bureau-Specific Variations
Each template should have versions for:
- Experian
- Equifax
- TransUnion
- Multi-bureau (all three)
