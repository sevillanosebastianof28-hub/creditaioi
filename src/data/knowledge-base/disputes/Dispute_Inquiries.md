### 2.1 Ineligible Dispute Scenarios

## 4. REQUIRED EVIDENCE

### 4.1 For Unauthorized Inquiry Disputes
- Written statement that no application was submitted
- Lack of account or correspondence from creditor
- Identity theft report (if applicable)
- Timeline showing impossibility (e.g., out of country)

### 4.2 For Unknown Company Disputes
- Documentation showing company is unrecognized
- Research showing company identity unclear
- Request for investigation into inquiry source

### 4.3 For Identity Theft Inquiries
- FTC Identity Theft Report
- Police report
- Fraud affidavit
- Documentation of identity theft

---

## 5. EXPECTED BUREAU RESPONSE TYPES

### 5.1 Possible Outcomes
| Response | Meaning | Next Steps |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Deleted | Inquiry removed | Success |
| Verified | Inquiring creditor confirmed | May need direct dispute |
| No Response | Creditor didn't respond | May result in deletion |
| Soft Conversion | Changed to soft inquiry | Success if appropriate |

### 5.2 Understanding Verification
- Creditor confirmed they made inquiry
- Does NOT mean inquiry was authorized
- May warrant direct dispute with creditor
- Consider escalation if truly unauthorized

---

## 6. ESCALATION RULES

### 6.1 When to Escalate
Escalation appropriate when:
- Have evidence inquiry was unauthorized
- Creditor cannot provide application
- Identity theft confirmed
- Bureau failed to investigate

### 6.2 Escalation Path
1. **Round 1**: Bureau dispute
2. **Round 2**: Direct creditor dispute for authorization proof
3. **Round 3**: CFPB complaint if creditor cannot prove authorization
4. **Beyond**: Legal consultation for FCRA violation

### 6.3 Escalation NOT Appropriate
- Inquiry was authorized
- Consumer applied for credit with company
- Inquiry is from legitimate account management
- Consumer simply regrets application

## 8. STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS

### 8.1 Prioritization
- Inquiries have LEAST score impact
- Focus on unauthorized inquiries only
- Don't waste effort on authorized ones
- Inquiries age off quickly (2 years)

### 8.2 For Recent Unauthorized Inquiries
1. Document lack of authorization immediately
2. File bureau dispute
3. Request proof of application from creditor
4. File identity theft report if applicable

### 8.3 For Old Inquiries (1+ years)
1. Will age off within 1 year
2. Minimal current score impact
3. May not be worth dispute effort
4. Focus on more impactful items

### 8.4 For Rate Shopping Inquiries
1. Multiple mortgage/auto inquiries in 14-45 days
2. Should count as single inquiry for scoring
3. All may appear but only one impacts score
4. Generally not worth disputing

## 9. INQUIRY SCORE IMPACT REALITY

### 9.1 Actual Impact
| Factor | Impact Level |
|--------|--------------|
| Single inquiry | 0-5 points typically |
| Multiple inquiries | May compound slightly |
| Rate shopping | Counted as one |
| After 12 months | Minimal to no impact |
| After 24 months | Removed from report |

### 9.2 Perspective
- Inquiries are LEAST important score factor
- Payment history (35%) matters far more
- Utilization (30%) matters far more
- Focus dispute efforts on bigger items first

### 9.3 When Inquiries Matter Most
- Many inquiries in short period
- Combined with other negative factors
- Borderline credit applications
- Thin credit file

---

## 10. SOFT VS HARD INQUIRIES

### 10.1 Hard Inquiries (Affect Score)
- Credit applications
- Loan applications
- Credit card applications
- Auto financing
- Mortgage applications
- Apartment rental (sometimes)

### 10.2 Soft Inquiries (No Score Impact)
- Personal credit checks
- Pre-approved offers
- Account reviews by existing creditors
- Employment screening (with consent)
- Insurance quotes
- Background checks

### 10.3 Dispute Focus
- Only hard inquiries affect score
- Only dispute unauthorized hard inquiries
- Soft inquiries don't need to be disputed
- Soft inquiries may not even appear on consumer report
