# Dispute Strategy: Closed Accounts

**Document Priority: HIGH**
**Last Updated: 2025-01-29**
**Account Type: Closed Accounts**

---

## 1. WHEN DISPUTE IS ALLOWED

### 1.1 Eligible Dispute Scenarios
✅ **Account shows open when closed**
- Consumer closed account
- Creditor closed account
- Report still shows "open" status

✅ **Closed date is incorrect**
- Wrong month/year of closure
- Affects account age calculations

✅ **Reason for closure is wrong**
- Shows "closed by creditor" when consumer closed
- Shows negative closure reason incorrectly
- Status implies negative when neutral

✅ **Balance showing on closed account**
- Paid in full but shows balance
- Settled but shows remaining balance
- Charged off but sold, should show $0

✅ **Late payments on closed account after closure**
- Shows late payment after account closed
- Payment history extends beyond closure

✅ **Account still reporting when should have aged off**
- Closed account beyond 10-year positive reporting
- Negative closed account beyond 7-year limit

✅ **Wrong account type or terms**
- Incorrect original credit limit
- Wrong account type classification
- Incorrect payment terms

---

## 2. WHEN DISPUTE IS NOT ALLOWED

### 2.1 Ineligible Dispute Scenarios
❌ **Accurate closed account information**
- Closed date is correct
- Status accurately reflects closure
- Balance (if any) is accurate

❌ **Disputing because account was closed by creditor**
- Creditors can close accounts for various reasons
- Closure by creditor is not inaccurate reporting
- May be negative but is reportable

❌ **Disputing to remove positive closed account**
- Positive closed accounts help credit
- Generally should remain on report
- Removal may hurt, not help

❌ **Disputing closed account simply to remove**
- Being closed is not an error
- Accurate closure info is reportable

---

## 3. RISK INDICATORS

### 3.1 High Risk (Extra Verification Needed)
⚠️ Consumer claims account shouldn't be closed
⚠️ Consumer disputes account they stopped using
⚠️ No documentation of closure request
⚠️ Account has outstanding balance
⚠️ Consumer confused about account status

### 3.2 Low Risk Scenarios
- Consumer has closure confirmation letter
- Consumer has $0 balance confirmation
- Clear discrepancy in closure date
- Status code clearly incorrect

---

## 4. REQUIRED EVIDENCE

### 4.1 For Status Disputes
- Letter confirming account closed
- Final statement showing closure
- Communication from creditor about closure
- Online account showing closed status

### 4.2 For Balance Disputes
- Final statement showing $0 balance
- Payoff confirmation
- Settlement letter
- Payment confirmation

### 4.3 For Closure Date Disputes
- Correspondence showing closure date
- Account statements around closure time
- Creditor confirmation of actual closure date

### 4.4 For Closure Reason Disputes
- Letter from creditor explaining closure
- Evidence consumer initiated closure
- Account history showing reason

---

## 5. EXPECTED BUREAU RESPONSE TYPES

### 5.1 Possible Outcomes
| Response | Meaning | Next Steps |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Updated | Information corrected | Verify changes |
| Verified | Creditor confirmed info | Review if accurate |
| Deleted | Account removed | May not be desired if positive |
| Modified | Partial changes | Check what changed |

### 5.2 Considerations
- Deletion of positive closed accounts hurts credit
- Focus on correcting info, not removal
- Old positive accounts help average age

---

## 6. ESCALATION RULES

### 6.1 When to Escalate
Escalation appropriate when:
- Have documentation proving inaccuracy
- Balance clearly wrong
- Status clearly incorrect
- Bureau didn't correct obvious error

### 6.2 Escalation Path
1. **Round 1**: Bureau dispute with documentation
2. **Round 2**: Direct creditor dispute + bureau escalation
3. **Round 3**: Executive escalation
4. **Round 4**: CFPB if necessary

### 6.3 Escalation NOT Appropriate
- Information is accurate
- Disputing accurate closure by creditor
- No documentation supporting claim
- Trying to remove positive account

## 7. COMPLIANCE WARNINGS

### 7.1 Credit AI Must
- ✅ Clarify closed accounts can be positive
- ✅ Explain closed account reporting rules
- ✅ Focus on accuracy, not removal
- ✅ Explain account age implications
- ✅ Be clear about what can be disputed

### 7.2 Credit AI Must NOT
- ❌ Advise removing positive closed accounts
- ❌ Suggest disputing accurate closure info
- ❌ Promise removal of closed accounts
- ❌ Imply all closed accounts should be disputed
- ❌ Ignore positive impact of old accounts

### 7.3 Required Disclaimers
> "Closed account disputes should focus on inaccurate information. Positive closed accounts benefit your credit history and should generally remain. Results cannot be guaranteed."

---

## 8. STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS

### 8.1 For Positive Closed Accounts
1. Generally, leave alone
2. Dispute only if information is wrong
3. Old positive accounts help credit age
4. Don't try to remove unless inaccurate

### 8.2 For Negative Closed Accounts
1. Focus on specific inaccuracies
2. Verify within reporting period
3. Check for balance errors
4. Dispute incorrect status codes

### 8.3 For "Closed by Creditor" Accounts
1. This is accurate if creditor closed it
2. Cannot dispute accurate reason
3. Focus on other inaccuracies if present
4. Consider if account was actually consumer-closed

### 8.4 For Paid-Off Closed Accounts
1. Verify shows $0 balance
2. Confirm shows "Paid" or "Closed"
3. Check for lingering late payments
4. Should reflect positive payment history

---

## 9. REPORTING RULES FOR CLOSED ACCOUNTS

### 9.1 Positive Closed Accounts
- Can remain on report for ~10 years after closure
- Helps credit history length
- Benefits credit mix
- Removal may hurt score

### 9.2 Negative Closed Accounts
- Must be removed 7 years from DOFD
- Late payments follow 7-year rule
- Charge-offs follow 7-year rule from original DOFD

### 9.3 Account Age Impact
- Closed accounts still count for average age
- Old closed accounts beneficial
- Losing old accounts hurts average age
- Consider before trying to remove
