# Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) Summary

**Document Priority: HIGHEST**
**Last Updated: 2025-01-29**
**Reference: 15 U.S.C. § 1681**

---

## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) is a federal law that:
- Promotes accuracy, fairness, and privacy of consumer information
- Regulates how consumer reporting agencies (CRAs) collect and share data
- Establishes consumer rights to access and dispute their credit information
- Defines obligations for furnishers (creditors, collectors) reporting data

### 1.1 Covered Entities
| Entity Type | FCRA Role |
|-------------|-----------|
| Credit Bureaus | Consumer Reporting Agencies (CRAs) |
| Creditors | Furnishers of information |
| Collectors | Furnishers of information |
| Employers | Users of consumer reports |
| Landlords | Users of consumer reports |
| Insurers | Users of consumer reports |

---

## 2. CONSUMER DISPUTE RIGHTS

### 2.1 Right to Dispute (§ 1681i)
Consumers have the RIGHT to:
- Dispute any information they believe is inaccurate
- Receive free investigation of disputed items
- Have inaccurate information corrected or deleted
- Receive written results of investigations

### 2.2 Dispute Process Timeline
| Stage | Timeframe |
|-------|-----------|
| Bureau receives dispute | Day 0 |
| Bureau notifies furnisher | Within 5 business days |
| Investigation period | 30 days (45 if additional info provided) |
| Results notification | Within 5 days of completion |

### 2.3 Bureau Obligations During Dispute
- Conduct reasonable investigation
- Review all relevant information submitted
- Forward dispute to furnisher
- Record current status of disputed item
- Delete or modify if found inaccurate
- Notify consumer of results in writing

---

## 3. FURNISHER ACCURACY REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Duty to Report Accurately (§ 1681s-2)
Furnishers MUST:
- Report accurate information to CRAs
- Correct and update reported information
- Investigate consumer disputes
- Not report information known to be inaccurate

### 3.2 Common Accuracy Violations
| Violation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Wrong DOFD | Incorrect Date of First Delinquency |
| Balance errors | Reporting wrong amount owed |
| Status code errors | Wrong account status (open/closed) |
| Duplicate reporting | Same debt reported twice |
| Identity mismatch | Wrong consumer associated |
| Date inconsistencies | Conflicting dates across bureaus |

### 3.3 Re-aging Prohibition
- Furnishers CANNOT re-age debts
- Date of First Delinquency (DOFD) must remain consistent
- 7-year reporting clock starts from DOFD
- Re-aging is a FCRA violation

---

## 4. BUREAU INVESTIGATION TIMELINES

### 4.1 Standard Investigation (§ 1681i(a)(1))
- **30 calendar days** from receipt of dispute
- Clock starts when bureau receives complete dispute
- Extensions allowed only with consumer additional info

### 4.2 Expedited Investigation
- **45 calendar days** if consumer provides additional information
- Within first 30 days of initial dispute

### 4.3 Frivolous Dispute Exception
Bureau may terminate investigation if dispute is:
- Substantially similar to previous dispute
- Does not provide sufficient identification
- Lacks specificity about disputed item

---

## 5. REPORTING TIME LIMITS (§ 1681c)

### 5.1 Standard Reporting Periods
| Information Type | Reporting Limit |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Late payments | 7 years from delinquency |
| Collections | 7 years from DOFD |
| Charge-offs | 7 years from DOFD |
| Judgments | 7 years from filing |
| Bankruptcies (Ch. 7/11) | 10 years from filing |
| Bankruptcies (Ch. 13) | 7 years from filing |
| Hard inquiries | 2 years from inquiry |
| Tax liens (paid) | 7 years from payment |

### 5.2 DOFD Definition
Date of First Delinquency (DOFD):
- The date the account first became delinquent
- AND was never brought current afterward
- This date CANNOT be changed by subsequent events
- Transfer to collections does not reset DOFD

---

## 6. VIOLATIONS VS NON-VIOLATIONS

### 6.1 What Constitutes a VIOLATION
- ❌ Reporting after 7-year limit expired
- ❌ Wrong DOFD causing extended reporting
- ❌ Failure to investigate dispute
- ❌ Re-inserting deleted information without notice
- ❌ Inaccurate balance, status, or dates
- ❌ Reporting debt not belonging to consumer

### 6.2 What is NOT a Violation
- ✅ Accurate negative information (even if harmful)
- ✅ Dispute verification with accurate data
- ✅ Bureau finding information verified
- ✅ Reporting within statutory time limits
- ✅ Accurate status updates

### 6.3 Important Distinction
> **Accurate negative information is LEGAL to report.**
> 
> Disputes should focus on INACCURACY, not negative impact.
> Credit AI must never advise disputing accurate information.

---

## 7. CONSUMER REMEDIES

### 7.1 Available Actions
- File complaint with CFPB
- File complaint with FTC
- Dispute directly with bureau
- Dispute with furnisher
- Seek legal counsel for willful violations

### 7.2 Statutory Damages (If Applicable)
- Actual damages (proven harm)
- Statutory damages ($100-$1,000 per violation)
- Punitive damages for willful violations
- Attorney's fees and costs

### 7.3 Credit AI Limitations
Credit AI:
- CANNOT advise on legal remedies
- CANNOT calculate potential damages
- CANNOT recommend litigation
- CAN explain consumer rights educationally
- CAN explain complaint filing processes
