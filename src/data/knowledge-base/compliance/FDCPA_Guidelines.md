# Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) Guidelines

**Document Priority: HIGHEST**
**Last Updated: 2025-01-29**
**Reference: 15 U.S.C. § 1692**

---

## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) is a federal law that:
- Prohibits abusive debt collection practices
- Establishes consumer rights when dealing with collectors
- Defines what debt collectors can and cannot do
- Provides remedies for violations

### 1.1 Who is Covered
| Covered | Not Covered |
|---------|-------------|
| Third-party debt collectors | Original creditors (generally) |
| Collection agencies | In-house collection departments |
| Debt buyers | Creditors using own name |
| Collection attorneys | Non-debt communications |

### 1.2 What Debts are Covered
- Consumer debts (personal, family, household)
- Credit card debt
- Medical debt
- Auto loans
- Mortgages
- Student loans (private)

NOT Covered:
- Business debts
- Commercial obligations
- Debts incurred for business purposes

## 2. DEBT VALIDATION RULES (§ 1692g)

### 2.1 Initial Validation Notice
Within 5 days of first communication, collector MUST provide:
- Amount of debt
- Name of creditor
- Statement of consumer's rights
- 30-day dispute window notice

### 2.2 Consumer's Right to Validate
Consumer may request validation within 30 days:
- Dispute validity of debt
- Request name and address of original creditor
- Request verification of debt

### 2.3 Collector Obligations Upon Validation Request
Collector MUST:
- Cease collection until verification provided
- Provide verification of debt
- Provide name/address of original creditor (if requested)

Collector MUST NOT:
- Continue collection during validation period
- Report to credit bureaus during dispute (debated)
- Threaten further action without verification

### 2.4 What Constitutes Valid Verification
Acceptable verification includes:
- Copy of signed agreement
- Account statements
- Bill or invoice
- Itemized accounting

NOT sufficient verification:
- Computer printout without backup
- Verbal confirmation
- Letter simply restating amount

## 3. COLLECTOR OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Communication Restrictions
| Prohibited | Allowed |
|------------|---------|
| Before 8 AM or after 9 PM | During reasonable hours |
| At workplace if prohibited | At home (with restrictions) |
| After cease contact request | With attorney if represented |
| Third-party disclosure | Direct consumer contact |

### 3.2 Prohibited Practices
Collectors CANNOT:
- Harass, oppress, or abuse
- Use obscene language
- Threaten violence
- Call repeatedly to annoy
- Misrepresent debt amount
- Claim to be attorneys (if not)
- Threaten arrest or imprisonment
- Collect unauthorized amounts
- Deposit post-dated checks early

### 3.3 Required Disclosures
Every communication must disclose:
- Collector is attempting to collect a debt
- Information will be used for that purpose
- (In initial communication) Mini-Miranda warning

---

## 4. CONSUMER RIGHTS

### 4.1 Right to Request Validation
- Available for 30 days after initial contact
- Pauses collection activity
- Requires written verification
- Applies to each new collector

### 4.2 Right to Cease Communication
Consumer may request in writing:
- Stop all contact
- Collector must comply (with exceptions)
- Exceptions: final notice, legal action notice

### 4.3 Right to Dispute
Consumer may dispute:
- Validity of debt
- Amount of debt
- Ownership of debt
- Identity (not their debt)

---

## 5. WHAT CONSUMERS MAY REQUEST

### 5.1 Validation Letter Contents
Consumer may request:
- Name of original creditor
- Amount owed (itemized)
- Copy of signed agreement
- Complete payment history
- Chain of assignment/sale
- License to collect in state

### 5.2 Sample Validation Request Language
```
I am requesting validation of this debt pursuant to 
15 U.S.C. § 1692g. Please provide:
1. Verification of the amount claimed
2. Name and address of original creditor
3. Copy of any judgment (if applicable)
4. Proof that you are licensed to collect in my state
5. Copy of the original signed agreement

Until you provide this validation, please cease 
all collection activity on this account.
```

## 6. WHAT CANNOT BE ADVISED

### 6.1 Credit AI Restrictions
Credit AI CANNOT advise consumers to:
- ❌ Ignore legitimate debts
- ❌ File false disputes
- ❌ Claim debts aren't theirs when they are
- ❌ Threaten collectors with lawsuits
- ❌ Record calls without proper consent
- ❌ Make false statements

### 6.2 Legal Boundaries
Credit AI CANNOT:
- Provide legal advice
- Recommend specific attorneys
- Calculate damages
- File complaints on behalf of consumers
- Negotiate debt settlements

### 6.3 What Credit AI CAN Do
- ✅ Explain consumer rights educationally
- ✅ Provide template validation requests
- ✅ Explain dispute processes
- ✅ Identify potential violations (educationally)
- ✅ Direct to CFPB/FTC complaint processes

## 7. VIOLATION INDICATORS

### 7.1 Potential FDCPA Violations
When collector:
- Fails to validate upon request
- Continues collection during validation period
- Uses abusive language
- Misrepresents amount owed
- Threatens illegal action
- Contacts at prohibited times
- Contacts employer inappropriately
- Discloses debt to third parties

### 7.2 Documentation Recommendations
Advise consumers to document:
- Date and time of all calls
- Name of collector and company
- What was said (quotes if possible)
- Witnesses present
- Written correspondence

---

## 8. INTEGRATION WITH CREDIT DISPUTES

### 8.1 Collection Account Disputes
When disputing collection accounts:
- Request validation from collector
- Dispute with credit bureaus
- Document all communications
- Track validation deadlines

### 8.2 Dispute Strategy Connection
- Validation request: 30-day window from first contact
- Bureau dispute: Anytime
- Combined approach may be effective
- Lack of validation may support bureau dispute
