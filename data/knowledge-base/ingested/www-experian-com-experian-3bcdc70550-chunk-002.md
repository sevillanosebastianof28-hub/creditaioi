Credit card points programs reward your spending with points that can be redeemed for various options, most commonly travel. When you make a purchase, you earn points based on your spending. Depending on the card, you may earn a flat rewards rate on all of your purchases or tiered rewards based on your spending categories. General rewards programs are tied to the card issuer and can be redeemed within their ecosystem or travel portal. Co-branded programs partner with specific airlines or hotels, earning points directly in those loyalty programs. Unlike with cash back cards, the value of credit card points can vary significantly based on how you redeem them. For example, some programs let you redeem points for statement credits, travel or gift cards, typically at a value of 1 cent per point or less. However, when you transfer points to airline and hotel partners, they can deliver significantly more value, sometimes reaching 1.5 to 2 cents per point or higher.
